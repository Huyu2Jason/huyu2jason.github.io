I am currently pursuing the Ph.D degree with School of Computer Science and Engineering, South China University of Technology, GuangZhou, China.

My research interests include machine learning, data mining, multi-source data fusion, as well as their applications such as high-dimensional data clustering, multi-modal / multi-view clustering, recommender systems, and phase retrieval.

In particular, my current research focuses on

  • Classifying high-dimensional data in an unsupervised manner
  • Integrating multi-sourced data in a robust way
  • Exploring high-order data relations for clustering

Selected Publications (* indicates the equal contribution authors and £ denotes the corresponding authors)

  • Graph Attention Network based Inductive Recommender System

    Kai Zhou*, Ran Yang*, Yu Hu*, Fei Liu£, Mingkui Tan£

    Chinese Journal of Computers (CCF Rank A)

    • Keywords: recommender system; graph neural network; graph attention network; inductive learning

    • Summary: This paper proposes a graph attention network method for inductive recommender system. A subgraph extraction approach based on sampling neighbors is proposed to achieve an inductive recommendation and reduce the dependency of user-specific content. A graph attention network with the edge embedding module is proposed to effectively learn the user/item representation for a better recommendation performance.

  • Phase Recovery With Deep Complex-Domain Priors

    Zhuojie Chen, Yan Huang, Yu Hu£, Zhifeng Chen£

    IEEE Signal Processing Letters (JCR Q2, Impact Factor: 3.109)

    • Keywords: complex-valued transform; deep learning; phase recovery; unrolled optimization

    • Summary: This paper proposes a deep model-aware approach for phase recovery (PR) by unrolling an optimization model regularized with image priors defined in the complex domain. A complex-valued (CV) deep neural network is then introduced to implement effective plug-and-play image priors that enjoy the benefits of CV operations for PR, such as sophisticated operations on local phases and regularization by compact convolution.

  • Multi-view Clustering through Hypergraphs Integration on Stiefel Manifold

    Yu Hu, Hongmin Cai£

    2022 IEEE International Conference on Multimedia and Expo (ICME) (CCF Rank B, Acceptance rate: 29%)

    • Keywords: multi-view clustering; manifold learning; data integration

    • Summary: A manifold-based integration is proposed to integrate multi-view (multi-source) data. The proposed method exploits the Stiefel manifold structure of multi-view data to robustly integrate them. Compared with previous methods, the proposed method has shown robustness against noise and improve the multi-view clustering performance.

  • Hypergraph-Supervised Deep Subspace Clustering

    Yu Hu, Hongmin Cai£

    Mathematics (JCR Q1, Impact Factor: 2.258)

    • Keywords: deep learning; computational intelligence; neural networks; deep subspace clustering; hypergraph

    • Summary: A hypergraph-supervised deep subspace clustering (H-DSC) method is proposed to address high-dimensional data clustering. H-DSC features a hypergraph module to enhance deep feature learning of the auto-encoder and enables a better clustering performance. Compared with its state-of-the-art competitors, H-DSC uniformly achieves performance improvement on several benchmark datasets.

  • Integrating Tensor Similarity to Enhance Clustering Performance

    Hong Peng, Yu Hu, Jiazhou Chen, Haiyan Wang, Yang Li, Hongmin Cai£

    IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence (JCR Q1, Impact Factor: 16.389)

    • Keywords: tensor similarity; Kronecker product; spectral clustering; under-sampled; imbalanced dataset; unsupervised learning

    • Summary: A new concept, high-order affinity matrix, is proposed to extract the high-order information from the affinity tensor. The high-order affinity matrix captures spatial information that serves as a robust complement for the pairwise similarity and boosts clustering performance. Extensive experiments demonstrated that the proposed high-order similarity significantly outperformed previous similarity-based methods on real-world datasets and it was capable of handling the clustering task over under-sampled and noisy datasets.
