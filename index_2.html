I am currently pursuing the Ph.D degree with School of Computer Science and Engineering, South China University of Technology, GuangZhou, China.

My research interests include machine learning, data mining, multi-source data fusion, as well as their applications such as high-dimensional data clustering, multi-view clustering, recommender systems, phase retrieval and medical image analysis.

In particular, my current research focuses on

  • Classifying high-dimensional data in an unsupervised manner
  • Integrating multi-sourced data in a robust way
  • Exploring high-order relations for underlying data structure discovery

Selected Publications

  • Hypergraph-Supervised Deep Subspace Clustering
    Yu Hu, Hongmin Cai
    Mathematics (JCR Q1, Impact Factor: 2.258)
    • Keywords: deep learning; computational intelligence; neural networks; deep subspace clustering; hypergraph
    • Summary: We propose a hypergraph-supervised deep subspace clustering (H-DSC) method to address high-dimensional data clustering. H-DSC features a hypergraph module to enhance deep feature learning of the auto-encoder and enables a better clustering performance. Compared with its state-of-the-art competitors, H-DSC uniformly achieves performance improvement on several benchmark datasets.
  • Integrating tensor similarity to enhance clustering performance
    Hong Peng, Yu Hu, Jiazhou Chen, Haiyan Wang, Yang Li, Hongmin Cai
    IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence (JCR Q1, Impact Factor: 16.389)
    • Keywords: tensor similarity; Kronecker product; spectral clustering; under-sampled; imbalanced Dataset; unsupervised Learning
    • Summary: We propose a new concept, high-order similarity, which are learned from the high-order adjacency tensor. The high-order similarity captures spatial information that serves as a robust complement for the pairwise similarity and boosts clustering performance. Extensive experiments demonstrated that the proposed high-order similarity significantly outperformed previous similarity-based methods on real-world datasets and it was capable of handling the clustering task over under-sampled and noisy datasets.
